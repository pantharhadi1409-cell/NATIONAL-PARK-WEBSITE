Fairy Meadows National Park

Fairy Meadows National Park — Pakistan

Fairy Meadows National Park is one of Pakistan’s most enchanting high-altitude destinations, located in the Gilgit-Baltistan region, near the base of Nanga Parbat, the world’s ninth-highest mountain (8,126 m). The park lies within the Karakoram-Himalaya ecosystem and covers an area of approximately 580 square kilometers (224 sq mi). Known for its breathtaking alpine meadows, lush forests, and panoramic mountain views, Fairy Meadows is often referred to as “Joot” locally, meaning beautiful meadows. The park was officially designated as a protected area to preserve its unique landscapes, biodiversity, and cultural significance. It has become a globally renowned destination for trekkers, photographers, and nature lovers seeking pristine wilderness and spectacular vistas. Fairy Meadows National Park plays a crucial role in protecting high-altitude ecosystems, preserving endangered species like the Snow Leopard and Himalayan Ibex, and maintaining fragile alpine vegetation. Its stunning landscapes also promote eco-tourism, helping local communities while conserving one of Pakistan’s most iconic natural wonders.

Established: 1995 Elevation: 3,300 metres (10,800 ft) Region: Diamer District,Gilgit-Baltistan, Pakistan

Wild Life

Wild Life Of Fairy Meadows National park

Fairy Meadows National Park is home to a variety of mammals, birds, and reptiles, adapted to its alpine environment: Himalayan Ibex – grazing across alpine slopes Snow Leopard – elusive predator of the region Red Fox, Pika, and Tibetan Wolf Markhor – occasionally spotted in surrounding areas The park is also a haven for birdlife, including Golden Eagles, Himalayan Monal, Snow Partridges, and other high-altitude species. Seasonal streams and forests support amphibians and small mammals, contributing to the park’s rich biodiversity.
