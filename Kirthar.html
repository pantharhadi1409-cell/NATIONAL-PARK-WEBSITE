Kirthar National Park

Kirthar National Park — Pakistan

Kirthar National Park is one of Pakistan’s largest and most ecologically significant protected areas, located in Sindh province, stretching across Dadu and Jamshoro districts. Established in 1974, the park covers an area of approximately 3,087 square kilometers (1,192 sq mi), making it a vital refuge for wildlife in Pakistan’s arid and semi-arid regions. The park lies within the Kirthar mountain range, featuring rugged hills, rocky outcrops, desert plains, and seasonal streams. Its diverse terrain creates a variety of habitats, supporting a rich mix of flora and fauna. The Kirthar landscape is marked by dramatic mountain ridges, dry riverbeds, and sparse forests of acacia and desert shrubs, offering a stark yet beautiful wilderness experience. Kirthar National Park is crucial for protecting arid-land biodiversity and endangered species like Sindh Ibex and Chinkara. Its mountains and desert plains serve as a natural buffer against desertification, support traditional pastoral communities, and offer opportunities for eco-tourism and scientific research.

Wild Life

Wild Life Of Kirthar National park

Kirthar National Park is home to some of the country’s most endangered and rare species, adapted to arid conditions: Key mammals include: Sindh Ibex (Capra aegagrus blythi) – the park’s iconic species Chinkara (Indian Gazelle) (Gazella bennettii) Urial (Wild Sheep) (Ovis vignei) Hyaena Wolf and Jackal Foxes and Small Carnivores The park is also a birdwatcher’s haven, with species such as: Partridges, Quails, and Sandgrouse Eagles and Falcons Migratory birds that visit seasonal wetlands
