Deosai National Park

Deosai National Park — Pakistan

Deosai National Park, also known as the Deosai Plains, is one of Pakistan’s most stunning high-altitude national parks, located in Skardu District, Gilgit-Baltistan, and extending into Astore District. Established in 1993, the park covers an area of approximately 3,000 square kilometers (1,160 sq mi), making it one of the largest plateaus in the world. The park sits at an average elevation of 4,114 meters (13,497 ft) above sea level and is famously known as the “Land of Giants” due to its vast open plains, rolling grasslands, and breathtaking alpine scenery. In summer, the park comes alive with wildflowers carpeting the meadows, while winter brings heavy snowfall and a quiet, pristine landscape. Deosai National Park is crucial for protecting high-altitude ecosystems, endangered species like the Himalayan brown bear, and rare alpine flora. Its vast, open plains also act as a climate buffer and support traditional grazing communities while attracting nature lovers and eco-tourists from around the world.

Wild Life

Wild Life Of Deosai National park

Deosai National Park is a critical sanctuary for endangered species and a haven for wildlife adapted to high-altitude ecosystems. Key species include: Himalayan Brown Bear (Ursus arctos isabellinus) – the park’s flagship species and one of the rarest bears in the world Marco Polo Sheep (Ovis ammon polii) Himalayan Ibex (Capra sibirica) Snow Leopard (Panthera uncia) Red Fox, Lynx, and Tibetan Wolf The park is also home to over 150 bird species, including Golden Eagles, Bearded Vultures (Lammergeier), Chukars, and migratory waterfowl that visit seasonal wetlands within the plateau. The diverse wildlife makes Deosai a global biodiversity hotspot.
