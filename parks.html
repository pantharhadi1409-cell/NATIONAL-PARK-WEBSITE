Explore Pakistan’s National Parks

From the snow-covered mountains of Khunjerab to the desert wonders of Hingol — Pakistan’s national parks are treasures of biodiversity and adventure.

Hingol National Park

A stunning mix of deserts, mountains, and coastline — home to unique rock formations, the Hingol River, and the famous “Princess of Hope”.

Learn More

Chitral Gol National Park

Located in Khyber Pakhtunkhwa, this park shelters the endangered markhor amidst breathtaking alpine meadows and snowy peaks.

Learn More

Khunjerab National Park

Pakistan’s highest national park, where snow leopards and Marco Polo sheep roam under the shadow of the Karakoram mountains.

Learn More

Central Karakoram National Park

Home to K2, glaciers, and alpine meadows — the largest protected area in Pakistan, rich with wildlife and mountain beauty.

Learn More

Deosai National Park

Known as the “Land of Giants”, Deosai is famous for its vast plains, Himalayan brown bears, and wildflower-filled landscapes.

Learn More

Margalla Hills National Park

A peaceful escape near Islamabad offering hiking trails, birdwatching, and panoramic views of the capital city.

Learn More

Kirthar National Park

Stretching across the rugged Kirthar Mountains of Sindh, this park is home to the Sindh ibex, urial, and chinkara gazelle — a haven for desert wildlife.

Learn More

Fairy Meadows National Park

Located in the heart of Gilgit-Baltistan, Fairy Meadows offers breathtaking views of Nanga Parbat and lush green meadows surrounded by snow-capped peaks.

Learn More
