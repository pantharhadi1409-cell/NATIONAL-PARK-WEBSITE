Margalla Hills National Park

Margalla Hills National Park — Pakistan

Margalla Hills National Park is a beautiful natural sanctuary located in the northern outskirts of Islamabad, Pakistan. Established in 1980, the park covers an area of approximately 12,605 hectares (126 km²), forming part of the Himalayan foothills ecosystem. Its proximity to the capital city makes it a unique blend of accessibility and wilderness, offering visitors the chance to explore lush forests, rocky hills, and scenic trails just minutes away from urban life. The park is characterized by rolling hills, deep valleys, and small streams, with elevations ranging from 500 to 1,600 meters above sea level. The terrain supports subtropical pine and oak forests, interspersed with scrub and grasslands, providing a haven for a rich variety of flora and fauna. Margalla Hills National Park protects one of the last remaining subtropical Himalayan foothill ecosystems near a major urban center. It provides refuge for endangered species like the leopard and pangolin, supports migratory birds, and preserves the ecological balance of the region. Its forests also act as carbon sinks and help maintain water and soil stability for Islamabad and surrounding areas.

Wild Life

Wild Life Of Margalla Hills National park

Margalla Hills National Park is home to a diverse range of mammals, birds, and reptiles, making it one of the most biodiverse parks near a major city in Pakistan. Key species include: Leopard and common leopard – top predators of the park Gray goral and wild boar Rhesus monkeys and Indian pangolin Porcupines, civets, and hedgehogs The park is also a birdwatcher’s paradise, hosting over 250 species of birds, including: Eagles, falcons, and hawks Partridges, woodpeckers, and thrushes Migratory species during winter
