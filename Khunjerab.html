Khunjerab National Park

Khunjerab National Park — Pakistan

Khunjerab National Park (KNP) is one of Pakistan’s most spectacular and highest-altitude protected areas, located in the Gilgit-Baltistan region along the northern border with China. Established in 1975, the park covers an area of approximately 2,270 square kilometers (880 sq mi) and lies within the Karakoram Range, near the famous Khunjerab Pass, at an elevation of over 4,500 meters (15,000 ft) — making it one of the highest national parks in the world. The park was originally created to protect the endangered Marco Polo sheep (Ovis ammon polii), a rare subspecies known for its distinctive spiral horns, as well as to conserve the unique high-mountain ecosystem of the Karakoram. Khunjerab’s landscape is a dramatic blend of snow-capped peaks, alpine meadows, glaciers, and high-altitude plateaus, surrounded by rugged rock formations and sweeping valleys. In summer, the region transforms into a lush alpine paradise filled with colorful wildflowers, while winter covers it in a blanket of snow.

Wild Life

Wild Life Of Khunjerab National park

Khunjerab National Park is home to some of Pakistan’s rarest and most endangered species adapted to extreme cold and altitude. Key mammals include: Marco Polo Sheep – the park’s flagship species Himalayan Ibex Snow Leopard – the elusive predator of the Karakoram Blue Sheep (Bharal) Tibetan Wolf Brown Bear Red Fox Pallas’s Cat Birds such as the Golden Eagle, Lammergeier (Bearded Vulture), Snow Partridge, and Himalayan Griffon soar over the high ridges, while migratory birds from Central Asia visit during the short summer season. This diverse wildlife makes Khunjerab one of the most important high-altitude biodiversity reserves in South Asia.
