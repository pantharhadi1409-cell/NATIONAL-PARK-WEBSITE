Karakoram National Park

Karakoram National Park — Pakistan

Karakoram National Park is one of Pakistan’s most awe-inspiring natural treasures, located in the Gilgit-Baltistan region of northern Pakistan. Encompassing the heart of the Karakoram mountain range, the park covers an immense area of approximately 10,000 square kilometers (3,900 sq mi) — making it one of the largest protected mountain areas in the country. The park is a land of towering peaks, glaciers, and rugged valleys, home to some of the world’s highest and most dramatic mountains — including K2 (8,611 m), the second-highest peak on Earth. It also contains three of the world’s seven longest glaciers outside the polar regions: the Baltoro, Biafo, and Siachen Glaciers, which feed major rivers and sustain life across northern Pakistan. The park’s terrain is characterized by steep granite mountains, icy glaciers, snowfields, and rocky moraines. At its lower elevations, alpine meadows and narrow valleys host small villages, while the higher regions remain untouched wilderness. The Baltoro Glacier region, surrounded by towering giants such as K2, Broad Peak, Gasherbrum I, and Gasherbrum II, forms one of the most breathtaking natural amphitheaters on Earth. Karakoram National Park lies within the Central Asian high-mountain ecosystem, linking the Karakoram, Himalaya, and Hindu Kush ranges — creating a globally significant biodiversity corridor.

Wild Life

Wild Life Of Karakoram National park

The park is home to an exceptional variety of high-altitude wildlife uniquely adapted to the cold, rugged environment. Among the most notable species are: Snow Leopard (Panthera uncia) – the apex predator of the Karakoram Marco Polo Sheep (Ovis ammon polii) Himalayan Ibex (Capra sibirica) Blue Sheep (Bharal) (Pseudois nayaur) Brown Bear (Ursus arctos isabellinus) Tibetan Wolf (Canis lupus chanco) Lynx, Red Fox, and Pikas Bird species include Golden Eagles, Bearded Vultures, Snow Partridges, and Himalayan Griffons, all thriving in the park’s high-altitude skies and cliffs.
